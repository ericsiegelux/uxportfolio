Introduction

Logos is a decentralized news platform for the new age of digital media.

I was brought on to the Logos design team to enhance the user experience of the main news feed. The CEO was looking for our team to conduct a complete redesign of the site. This involved building a new style guide for the site to use for our individual designs.

Problem Statement

After being onboarded with Logos and the design team, the problem statement became clear: How could I strike a balance between conventional design while showcasing Logos' innovative features?

My Research

I looked into the top 10 web app news sources (source). Here are some quick notes on the common themes and patterns across these sites:

8 out of the 10 sites had a navigation bar with different sections (sports, business, etc.)

Borders are dictated by margins, margin lines. If you zoom in, you can see light gray lines creating space for the articles and sections, but the lines that are perpendicular to each other never touch. The articles act as their own entities, but the sites display a level of cohesiveness in their proximity. You will also notice there is a mix of sizing and positioning with articles, especially with the featured section of news publications. Some are horizontal and some are vertical.

The article “tiles” have three main components of an article I found across the top websites are:

  1. Thumbnail
  2. Title
  3. Subheading (a sentence or two long)

As seen with the New York Times above, the featured article sections of most popular news sites are skeuomorphic in that they resemble a physical newspaper with an asymmetrical layout. For subsections of a site, it becomes a toss-up between a symmetrical layout (typically with horizontal article tiles) and an asymmetrical layout.

Moving Forward

I have taken these suggestions above and worked with the web development team to implement quick changes. At the time of writing this, I am continuing to work on improving the UX design and UI design of the homepage from those user interviews.

As I conclude this case study and continue working for Logos going into Fall 2020, I am also working on Logos' profile redesign and article redesign, as well as improving the homepage to reflect Logos' mission to deliver news transparently.
